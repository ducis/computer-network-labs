Textbooks

The official texts for the course is:

  • Computer Networking: A Top-Down Approach Featuring the Internet, 4th edition, by James F. Kurose and Keith W. Ross.

Some reference books are:

  • Computer Networks: A Systems Approach, third edition, by Larry Peterson and Bruce Davie.
  • TCP/IP Illustrated, Volume 1: The Protocols by W. Richard Stevens.
  • Unix Network Programming: Networking APIs: Sockets and XTI (Volume 1) by W. Richard Stevens.
  • Advanced Programming in the Unix Environment by W. Richard Stevens, Addison-Wesley, 1993.

Grading

Your final grade for the course will be based on the following weights:

  • 45% Homework assignments
  • 15% Labs & Class Participation
  • 40% Final Exam

There will be a few written and programming assignments. Each assignment should be completed individually.
